# Joli — Idea

## What it is

Joli is a family admin assistant for working parents. It connects to your inbox and calendar, reads the noise, and surfaces only what actually needs your attention — forms, deadlines, appointments, RSVPs, and the communication that runs a household.

The core insight: managing a family is a second full-time job. Most of it is invisible — it lives in your head, not on any list. Joli takes that mental load and handles it.

## Who it's for

Working parents. Specifically the person in the household who ends up being the "family operating system" — the one who remembers the permission slip, books the dentist, RSVPs to the birthday party, and follows up with the teacher.

## The problem

Family admin is endless, low-stakes individually, but high-stakes when it falls through. It doesn't come from one place — it arrives via email, school portals, group chats, and paper notes. There's no system that captures all of it. So it lives in your head, creating constant low-grade anxiety.

## What Joli handles

- School forms, permission slips, and messages that need action
- Daycare, teacher, and activity communication
- Doctor appointments and routine reminders
- Birthday RSVPs and weekend logistics
- The deadlines and details that usually live in your head

## How it works

1. **Connect once** — Link your inbox and calendar. That's it.
2. **Joli keeps watch** — Joli reads your inbox and flags what actually needs action.
3. **You stay present** — You hear about it when it matters. Nothing falls through.

## The dashboard

The product surface is a focused daily dashboard, not a calendar or a to-do app. Three sections:

- **Needs your attention** — Action items with source, category, and due date. Numbered, prioritised, clearable.
- **Coming up** — The next few events and deadlines in plain language.
- **Handled by Joli** — A log of what Joli already took care of, for peace of mind.

No calendar grid. No inbox firehose. Just: here's what needs you today.

## The agentic vision

The current dashboard delivers half the promise. "Let this part be handled" means Joli should eventually do the work, not just flag it.

The model is **approval-gated agency** — not full autonomy. Joli proposes, the parent confirms with one tap, Joli executes. The parent never opens the school portal.

```
Joli detects permission slip due → pre-fills form with stored family data
Push: "Sign Luca's permission slip for Science Museum? Confirm · Review"
Parent taps Confirm → Joli submits → done.
```

This is different from scheduling assistants (like Clara) because the actions involve children — RSVPs, forms, medical appointments, payments. Full autonomy is the wrong model. Approval-gated earns trust incrementally.

**Build order:**
1. Extract + surface tasks (beta) — validate that Joli reads things correctly
2. One-tap RSVP from notification — lowest-effort write action
3. Pre-fill + submit simple forms using stored family profile data
4. Appointment booking, payments (later)

The evidence snippet and tap-to-detail being built for beta are the trust foundation. Parents need to trust Joli's extraction before they'll let it act on their behalf. The product is building in the right order.

## Positioning

- Not a calendar app (you already have one)
- Not a task manager (you don't want another list)
- Not a family organisation app (too broad)

Joli is a **family admin agent** — it reads the world on your behalf, tells you what needs doing, and increasingly handles it for you.
